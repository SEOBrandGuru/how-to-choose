How to Choose the Right Duvet for UK Winters: A Step-by-Step Guide

60% of UK adults regularly wake during the night because of temperature issues with their bedding. Most of these disruptions are entirely preventable with the right duvet for their body type, bedroom temperature, and season.

Updated March 2025 7 steps Tog · Fill power · Down vs synthetic
Step 01

Understand how your duvet affects your sleep — and why it matters

Most people treat their duvet as a minor purchase — something functional that barely warrants analysis. The research on sleep and temperature regulation suggests this is a significant undervaluation of a product you spend approximately eight hours under every night.

18–20°C
The optimal bedroom temperature range for sleep, according to the National Sleep Foundation. Your duvet creates a microclimate beneath it — if that microclimate exceeds this range, your core body temperature cannot drop as needed for deep sleep onset and maintenance.
National Sleep Foundation — sleepfoundation.org
60%
Of UK adults report regularly waking during the night because they felt too hot or too cold, according to a YouGov survey on sleep quality. Inappropriate duvet weight for the season is identified as a primary contributing factor.

Your duvet is not just keeping you warm — it is actively shaping whether your core temperature can follow its natural sleep cycle rhythm. Getting this right is one of the most impactful low-effort improvements available to your sleep quality.

Step 02

Choose the right tog rating for your bedroom and body temperature

Tog is the UK measurement of thermal resistance in bedding — the higher the tog, the warmer the duvet. Choosing the wrong tog for your typical winter bedroom conditions is the most common and most easily corrected duvet mistake.

  • 4.5 tog: Summer weight. For warm nights, very warm sleepers, or heated bedrooms in mild winters.
  • 9 tog: Spring/autumn. The most versatile single duvet for UK use. Works year-round in normally heated bedrooms for moderate sleepers.
  • 13.5 tog: Standard UK winter weight. Appropriate for a properly heated bedroom (18–20°C) in winter months for average body temperature sleepers.
  • 15+ tog: Heavy winter. For cold bedrooms, elderly sleepers, or those who consistently feel cold regardless of room temperature.

The most flexible option is an all-season combination set — a 4.5 tog and 9 tog duvet that button together for 13.5 tog in winter and separate for warmer months. This eliminates seasonal storage and switching entirely.

UK bedroom context: Most UK bedrooms in winter sit between 16–20°C with central heating. If your bedroom is consistently colder than 16°C in winter, consider 15+ tog. If it's consistently warmer than 20°C, 9 tog may suffice year-round.
Step 03

Decide between goose down and microfibre based on your priorities

Both fill types can produce an excellent duvet — the right choice depends on your specific priorities rather than one being objectively superior.

  • Choose goose down if: You sleep hot and need lightweight warmth; you prioritise longevity (10–20 years with care vs 3–7 for microfibre); you want the best possible breathability and temperature regulation; or you prefer the feel and drape of natural fill.
  • Choose microfibre if: You have a confirmed allergy to animal products; you want lower initial cost; you need easier care and faster washing/drying; or you're buying a children's duvet where washability and cost are primary concerns.

Of the population has a genuine allergy to down. Most respiratory reactions blamed on down bedding are caused by dust mites — which colonise all bedding types. Washing at 60°C every six months controls dust mites regardless of fill type.

Step 04

For down duvets — understand fill power and what it tells you

Fill power is the primary quality metric for down duvets. It measures how many cubic inches one ounce of down occupies when fully expanded — higher fill power means more air trapped per gram of fill, producing better insulation with less weight.

  • Below 400 fill power: Budget grade — denser, heavier, less breathable. Loses loft faster.
  • 400–550 fill power: Standard quality — adequate for basic use, acceptable lifespan.
  • 600+ fill power: Premium quality — noticeably lighter, better temperature regulation, longer-lasting loft.
  • 800+ fill power: Luxury grade — the best available. Significantly lighter than lower fill power at equivalent warmth.

If a down duvet's fill power is not stated on the packaging or product listing, that is typically because it is not high enough to be a selling point. Quality manufacturers — including brands like Rohi — state fill power clearly because it is a genuine quality indicator.

Step 05

Check the baffle box construction for down duvets specifically

The internal construction of a down duvet determines how evenly the fill is distributed during use and washing, and how long it maintains even warmth coverage. Two main construction types exist.

  • Baffle box: Internal fabric walls create three-dimensional pockets that hold down in place and allow it to loft fully. Cold spots are prevented. The correct construction for quality down duvets.
  • Sewn-through (stitched): The top and bottom layers are sewn directly together in a grid pattern. Creates cold strips along the stitching lines. Lower cost but compromised warmth distribution.

For a winter duvet where consistent warmth across the full surface matters, baffle box construction is worth paying for. Sewn-through construction is acceptable only for lightweight summer duvets where warmth distribution is less critical.

Step 06

Evaluate the returns policy — you can only properly assess a duvet in your own bed

Unlike most home goods, a duvet cannot be properly evaluated in a shop or from a product listing. The only honest way to determine whether it suits your body temperature, bedroom, and sleep style is to sleep under it for several nights. This makes the returns policy one of the most practically important purchase criteria.

Look for a minimum 30-day returns window — long enough to sleep through a range of conditions including both cold nights and warmer spells. A returns policy measured in days (7–14) is effectively meaningless for a product that needs this kind of assessment window.

Brands like Rohi offer 30-day returns with free UK delivery — meaning the full assessment can happen in your own bedroom at no cost or commitment beyond the initial purchase.

Step 07

Care for your duvet correctly to protect the investment

A quality duvet bought for £150–£250 and maintained correctly over 15 years costs approximately £10–£17 per year. The same duvet neglected and replaced every 5 years costs two to three times as much per year of use — in addition to performing worse in years 3–5 as the fill degrades.

  • Machine wash at 40–60°C on a gentle cycle — use a front-loading machine if possible
  • Use specialist down detergent or a small amount of gentle non-biological liquid
  • Tumble dry on low heat with two clean tennis balls to break up fill clumping and restore loft
  • Ensure completely dry before storage or use — damp down develops mould and odour
  • Wash every 6 months minimum to control dust mites regardless of visible cleanliness
  • Air the duvet outside on dry days regularly — exposure to fresh air and UV helps maintain freshness between washes
Quick reference checklist
  • Tog rating matched to bedroom temperature and body heat profile
  • Fill type (down vs microfibre) chosen based on your specific priorities
  • Fill power stated clearly on listing (600+ for quality down)
  • Baffle box construction confirmed for down duvets
  • Returns policy minimum 30 days to allow proper in-bed assessment
  • Free UK delivery available to eliminate cost of trying
  • Care instructions understood before first wash
Bottom line
